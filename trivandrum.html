THIRUVANTHAPURAM


  • Trivandrum or Thiruvananthapuram is the capital city of Kerala. Located on the western coastal regions of India, this metropolis is also the IT hub of God’s Own Country and one of the most sought after tourist destinations in Kerala
  • Once cited as the ‘Evergreen City of India’ by Mahatma Gandhi, Trivandrum has a diverse history and is a home to some of the most colourful and striking cultures. During the ancient times, the city had served as a major spice trading point among several Asian countries.
  • The city is built across seven of the most captivating hills and is dotted with several pious temples and religious sites. Though the city witness a number of rulers in its past, it has endured its beauty and liveliness in the most splendid ways, and has become of the highest visited tourist destinations in Kerala.

PLACES TO VISIT IN THIRUVANANTHAPURAM

  1. Padmanabhapuram Palace: The once an abode of the royal families of Travancore, Padmanabhapuram Palace is among the most well-preserved historic sites in Kerala. Built in traditional Kerala architecture, the palace is decked with exquisite murals, classy granite floors, livelier floor carvings, and wooden architecture.

  2. Varkala Beach: One of the virgin beaches in Kerala, Varkala is an ultimate destination to unwind and rejuvenate while enjoying the prismatic beauty of the Arabian Sea. Compared to other popular beaches in Kerala, Varkala Beach is less crowded and makes it for a serene beach experience.

  3. Ponmudi: This is the destination for those who yearn to spend some salubrious moments amidst the gorgeous hills of God’s Own Country. A scenic hill station in Kerala, it is perched at a height of 915m, and is a treasure trove for the nature lovers as well.

  4. Veli Tourist Village: Located at the confluence point of the Arabian Sea and the Veli Lake, this village is known for its bountiful beauty. While visiting the Veli hamlet, visitors can indulge in boating, village tour, and beach activities.

  5. Napier Museum: If you want to revisit the history of Kerala, you must visit the Napier Museum in Trivandrum. One of the oldest museums in the entire state, is has a large collection of bronze artefacts, ivory carvings, ancient musical instruments, and other traditional items.

  6. Poovar Island: Once a trading centre, Poovar Island has now become one of the most sought after tourist attractions in Trivandrum. Located on the southernmost tip of Kerala’s capital city, it is a wonderful day-out destination.

  7. Shanghumukham Beach: This quaint and equally charming beach is located away from the humdrums of city-life. Cossetted by the azure water and playful sand, it is a perfect place to sit back and relax while enjoying the pristine beauty of Mother Nature.

  8. Neyyer Dam: Situated at the foothills of the mighty Western Ghats, this dam is among the highest visited tourist attractions in Trivandrum. Adventure junkies can also visit this dam and indulge in exciting activities like boating and mountaineering.

  9. Neyyar Wildlife Sanctuary: For the wildlife lovers to Trivandrum, they must visit the Neyyar Wildlife Sanctuary which is around 35km from the heart of the city. A natural abode to exotic wildlife, it is known for its significant Lion, Elephant, Tiger, Leopard, Nilgiri Tahr, and several other endangered species.
